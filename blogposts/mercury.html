Fascinating Facts About Mercury

  • Speedy Gonzales: Mercury is the fastest planet in our solar system, zipping around the Sun every 88 Earth days at nearly 29 miles per second! That's faster than any other planet.
  • Hot and Cold: Despite being closest to the Sun, Mercury isn't the hottest planet. That title belongs to Venus. However, Mercury experiences wild temperature swings. On the sunlit side, temperatures soar to 800 degrees Fahrenheit (430 degrees Celsius), hot enough to melt lead! But on the night side, temperatures plummet to -290 degrees Fahrenheit (-180 degrees Celsius).
  • Cratered Companion: Mercury's surface is covered in craters, much like our moon, because it has no atmosphere to protect it from impacts by asteroids and comets.
  • Sunburnt Superman: The Sun appears three times larger and ten times brighter on Mercury than it does on Earth. You'd definitely need some serious sunscreen!
  • Day Longer Than a Year: A strange thing about Mercury is that a day on Mercury is longer than a year! It takes 59 Earth days for Mercury to rotate on its axis, but only 88 Earth days to orbit the Sun.
  • Shrinking Planet: Mercury is slowly shrinking! The tiny planet cools and contracts at a rate of about a millimeter per year.
  • Iron Giant: Mercury has a huge iron core that makes up about 75% of the planet's diameter. That's a much larger proportion of iron than any other planet in our solar system! Scientists are still trying to figure out why.
