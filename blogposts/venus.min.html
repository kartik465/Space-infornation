Fascinating Facts About Venus

  • Earth's Twin: Venus is often called Earth's twin because of its similar size and composition. However, its atmosphere and surface conditions are vastly different.
  • Hottest Planet: Venus holds the title for the hottest planet in our solar system with surface temperatures reaching up to 900 degrees Fahrenheit (475 degrees Celsius). This is due to its thick atmosphere of carbon dioxide and sulfuric acid clouds that trap heat.
  • Retrograde Rotation: Venus rotates in the opposite direction to most planets, meaning if you were standing on Venus, you would see the Sun rise in the west and set in the east.
  • Short Days: A day on Venus (one full rotation) is shorter than a year on Venus. It takes only about 243 Earth days to complete one rotation, while it takes about 225 Earth days to orbit the Sun.
  • Thick Atmosphere: Venus has an incredibly dense atmosphere made primarily of carbon dioxide, with clouds of sulfuric acid. This thick atmosphere creates a strong greenhouse effect.
  • Brightest Planet: Venus is one of the brightest objects in the sky after the Sun and the Moon. It is often visible from Earth around dawn and dusk and is known as the "Evening Star" or "Morning Star."
  • Volcanic Surface: Venus has many volcanoes, with some possibly still active. Its surface is covered with lava plains, and it's believed that volcanic activity has resurfaced much of the planet.
